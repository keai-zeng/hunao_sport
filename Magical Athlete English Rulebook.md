# Magical Athlete Rulebook

## Introduction

**Magical Athlete is a racing game of pure chaos!**

Roll a die and move your racer that many spaces
But all the racers have wacky, game-breaking abilities

Race four times— then whoever has the most points wins!

---

## SETUP

01. Put the racetrack in the middle of the table, Mild Mile side up.
02. Dump out the bag of point chips. Stack the gold trophy and silver rosette point chips in order, biggest on the bottom.
03. Keep the shuffled racer deck nearby!
04. Dump out the bag of racers.
05. Give everyone a die.

---

## RECRUIT YOUR TEAM

To start, everyone drafts a team of 4 racers that they'll use for the entire game, one racer per race.

Flip up twice as many racer cards as there are players and put them in a line in the middle of the table.
- 3 PLAYERS FLIP 6 CARDS
- 4 PLAYERS FLIP 8 CARDS
- 5 PLAYERS FLIP 10 CARDS
- 6 PLAYERS FLIP 12 CARDS

Racing with only 2 players? See page 26.

Now roll off! Everyone rolls their die. Whoever rolls highest drafts first! If there's a tie, the player with the next highest roll drafts first. If everyone ties, reroll.

Going clockwise, everyone picks a racer. When the last player picks, reverse the draft order: the last player picks a second racer, and now go counterclockwise until everyone has two racers. That's right, it's a snake draft!

Repeat this process, starting with the player to the left of the start player. That's right, it's a second snake draft! Now everybody has 4 racers total.

(If this all sounds too complicated for your group, or if you have a lot of new players, just deal everyone 4 random racers for their first game and learn on the fly. Or do the draft, but put out the racers instead of the cards, so people can decide entirely on vibes!)

---

## RACE!

Before the first race, roll off exactly like you did for the draft. Whoever wins goes first. Everyone picks a racer card from their hand and reveals it at the same time. You can only use each racer once, and later races are worth more points, so you might want to save your best racers for the end. Everyone takes their racer token from the pile and puts it onto the Start space.

Races are simple: players take turns rolling their dice, moving their racers, and watching the chaos unfold as powers trigger!

On your turn, roll your die and move that many spaces on the track. That's called your **main move**. That's a concept that's so important that we put it in bold and underlined it. A lot of powers trigger off the main move specifically, so keep an eye out for that wording, since racers can move in lots of other ways than their main moves.

After a player finishes their turn, the next player goes in clockwise order. Keep going until two racers cross the finish line. The first across takes 1st place, and when the second across takes 2nd place, the race ends immediately.

If a power ever says you CAN use it, it's optional. All other powers are mandatory!

### Power Trigger Order

A racer's power can take effect when a player chooses to use it, when a trigger occurs, or just passively. If multiple powers need to take effect at the same time, they activate in this order:

**RACETRACK SPACES → CURRENT PLAYER → OTHER PLAYERS (Going clockwise)**

### EXAMPLE

It's the Romantic's turn, and they roll a 5 for their main move. While they move 5 spaces, they pass Scoocher and Banana.

Banana's power is to trip any racer that passes them, so after the Romantic finishes their full main move, they trip! That means they skip their next main move.

Scoocher is thrilled, because their power is moving 1 whenever anyone's ability happens. So they move 1 and stop on the same space as Banana.

Now the Romantic is thrilled, because their ability is that when exactly 2 racers are stopped on a space, they get to move 2. Even though they're tripped, they can still use their power.

Of course, since another power is triggered, Scoocher moves another 1.

## AFTER THE RACE

As soon as the 2nd place racer finishes, the race ends!

The 1st place racer takes the top gold chip from the pile.
The 2nd place racer takes the top silver. Then...

01. Take all the cards and racer tokens from the race and put them out of play.
02. Flip the track over to the other side. (You'll race on each track twice per game: Mild, Wild, Mild, Wild.)
    If you prefer, you can race all on a single track, or change the schedule to your liking. Just decide before you start the game so you can strategize around it.
03. Everyone simultaneously reveals a new racer card and puts that racer token on the Start space. The player with the farthest behind (or first eliminated) racer in the last race goes first in the next race.

## FIDDLY DETAILS

Magical Athlete is a simple game, but the interactions can get wild! It helps to know a few key concepts. But don't stress—most of this will come up naturally as you play.

To the right, you'll find a quick guide to important terms, what happens on the track, and how certain racers work. If something unexpected happens, check here or just roll with it. Embrace the chaos and rules discussions—it's all part of the fun!

And if you're ever completely stumped, roll off to decide who's right.

### TERMS

**Ahead/Behind:**
Racers are ahead of another racer if they're stopped on a space closer to the finish line. Racers are behind another racer if they are stopped on a space closer to the Start space. If racers share a space, they're neither ahead nor behind each other.

**Sharing a space:**
Racers are "on" a space with another racer when both are currently stopped on that space. In other words, if racers temporarily occupy the same space over the course of moving, warping, etc.—that doesn't count!

**Arrows:**
When you stop on a space with an arrow, move the number of spaces shown in the arrow's direction. This counts as a separate move than how you got there, and never part of your main move, if it matters.

**Space:**
The squares beginning with the Start space and ending at the finish line. The Start space counts as a space! Anything past the finish line doesn't.

**Alone:**
When a racer isn't sharing their space.

**Trip:**
When you stop on a space that says TRIP, you trip!

**Last place:**
The racer(s) currently closest to the Start space.

**Stopping on a space:**
Racers are stopped on a space after they've finished moving onto it, or otherwise arriving there by other means, like through Flip Flop's warping power.

**Lead:**
The racer(s) currently closest to the finish line. This doesn't count racers who have already crossed the finish line.

**Star:**
When you stop on a space with a star, take a bronze 1 point chip.

**Main move:**
The action you always get on your turn, where you roll your die and move that many spaces.

**Trip:**
A tripped racer skips their next main move! You don't even roll your die, though your powers can still trigger and you can still move in other ways. When you trip, finish your move completely, then lay your racer token face down. On your next main move, put your token back upright to indicate you've recovered. And remember: tripping doesn't end your current move prematurely!

**Move:**
When a racer changes spaces from their main move, powers, or any other situation that specifically uses the word "move." If a racer ever needs to "move 0," that doesn't count as moving for the purposes of triggering other powers based on movement.

**Warp:**
When a racer warps, put their token on the new space, but don't count it as "moving" for triggering powers, passing racers, etc.

**Passing:**
When a racer starts a move behind a racer and ends the same move ahead of them.

### Additional Rules

1. If you reroll for any reason, treat the previous number you rolled as if it never happened. The reroll action itself would still trigger powers like Scoocher.
2. The Start space is a space!
3. Powers related to stopping on a space together still trigger if racers arrive at the same time.
4. Moving 0 doesn't count as moving.
5. Racer powers that happen at a specific time, like "Before my main move," only happen once per turn.
6. Racer powers deactivate after they win or otherwise leave a race, unless otherwise stated.
7. Any time you can reroll, that only means the most recent die you've rolled.
8. If you run into an infinite loop, e.g. Scoocher stopping on the Huge Baby, complete the loop once in the order it takes place, then end it. Some powers can interact many times, like Scoocher and Suckerfish, which doesn't count as a loop. You can keep doing those!
9. If racer powers create a loop where no one can finish, the race ends with no one getting the remaining points. Usually this can be resolved though!

## RACER INFO

### Alchemist
Forever seeking the ancient secret of turning This into That.

**Power:** When I roll a 1 or 2 for my main move, I can move 4 instead.
**Power Name:** TRANSMUTE 'N' SCOOT

### Blimp
Built to fly, born to explode.

**Power:** When I start my turn before the second corner of the track, I get +3 to my main move. On or after that corner, I get -1.
**Power Name:** BLOW IT

### Coach
Believing in yourself is even more important when you are some kind of lizard.

**Power:** Everyone on my space gets +1 to their main move, including me.
**Power Name:** GOOD HUSTLE

*I get my power's benefit too!*

### Baba Yaga
This property is going fast.

**Power:** Trip any racer that stops on my space, or when I stop on theirs.
**Power Name:** LEGIT

*If I share a space with the Duelist, we can duel, but the Duelist will still trip.*
*The Hypnotist can cast their spell on me, but they still trip.*

### Centaur
Here's the kicker.

**Power:** When I pass a racer, they move -2.
**Power Name:** HOOFWHACK

*A racer can't be hoofwhacked any farther back than the Start space.*

### Copy Cat
Very similar to another, different cat.

**Power:** I have the power of the racer currently in the lead. If there's a tie, I pick.
**Power Name:** COPY THAT

*I persistently have the power of the lead racer, not just on my turn. If there's ever a contradiction between me and the racer I'm copying, their power takes priority. I never copy "before my race" powers. If there's a tied lead, I can't switch my power mid-action.*

### Banana
No more edible than you or I.

**Power:** I trip any racer that passes me.
**Power Name:** THE SLIP

### Cheerleader
A being of pure pep.

**Power:** Before my main move, I can make the racer(s) in last place move 2. If I do, I move 1.
**Power Name:** RAH RAH

*If there is more than one racer in last place, they all move. But I only move +1 regardless of how many racers are in last.*
*If I cheer for myself, those are two separate moves: move 2 then move 1.*

### Dicemonger
He picked his career from a d100 table.

**Power:** Anyone can reroll their main move once per turn. When another racer does it, I move 1.
**Power Name:** DICEY DEALS

*If someone else rerolls, I move before they move.*

### Duelist
When you've got a rapier, everything looks like a duel.

**Power:** Whenever a racer shares my space, I can shout DUEL! We roll our dice and whoever rolls highest moves 2. I win ties.
**Power Name:** DUEL!

*I can duel multiple times in a turn, including when it's not my turn. I just need to be sharing a space to immediately declare a duel. I can't interrupt actions in progress, like between a roll and a move.*

### Genius
And yet a fool in the ways of love.

**Power:** I can predict what number I'll roll for my main move. If I'm right, I take another turn after this one.
**Power Name:** THINK GOOD

### Heckler
It's constructive criticism.

**Power:** When a racer ends their turn within 1 space of where they started, I move 2.
**Power Name:** SCHADENFREUDE

*My power works for racers recovering from tripping.*

### Egg
You should see the chicken.

**Power:** Before my race, draw 3 new racers from the deck and pick one. I have its powers.
**Power Name:** SCRAMBLE

*You should still use my racer token! I still get any "before race" powers.*

### Gunk
A bit of a hugger.

**Power:** Other racers get -1 to their main move.
**Power Name:** GOOP 'EM

*My goop reduces the move amount, not the die roll number. So it doesn't make a 6 into a 5 and keep the Lackey from doing their job.*

### Huge Baby

**Power:** No one can ever be on my space, besides the Start. Whenever that would happen, put the racer on the space behind me instead.
**Power Name:** REALLY HUGE

**Power Name:** FLOP FLIP

**Power Name:** SALTY DOG

Ope, just gonna scooch by you there.

Proofread this rulebook without asking.

**Power:** Other racers can only cross the finish line by moving the exact number of spaces they need. If they overshoot, they don't move.
**Power Name:** ACTUALLY...

Has abandonment issues.

**Power Name:** SUCKER!

*I can cross the finish line using my power.*

### Third Wheel
Searching for her throther half.

**Power Name:** ROLL THROUGH

*I still get my main move after warping.*

**Power Name:** DOUBLE DIP

## 2 PLAYER VARIANT

The rules are the same, except…

### Recruiting:
Make a line of 8 faceup racers from the deck and snake draft them (Players A and B would have a draft order like this: ABBAABBA) Do it again with 8 more racers from the deck, in reverse order, until both players have 8 racers (16 total between both of you).

### Preparing:
Both players simultaneously pick 2 different racers for each race, so 4 racers will be on the track to start. Each player also takes two dice to use for their different racers.

### Racing:
When a player goes, they take turns with both of their racers, one after the other, in whichever order they prefer. Just be sure to complete the first racer's turn before moving onto the next. If you have racer powers that trigger when "other racers" do something, that includes your second racer.

After the first race, the player who received the lower number of points in the last race goes first. If tied, roll off!

## 3 PLAYER DOUBLE RACER VARIANT

The rules are the same, except…

### Recruiting:
Make a line of 6 faceup racers from the deck and snake draft them like normal. Do that 4 times, rotating who drafts first. Everyone should have 8 racers after this.

### Preparing:
All players simultaneously pick 2 different racers for each race, so 6 racers will be on the track to start. Each player also takes two dice to use for their different racers.

### Racing:
When a player goes, they take turns with both of their racers, one after the other, in whichever order they prefer. Just be sure to complete the first racer's turn before moving onto the next. If you have racer powers that trigger when "other racers" do something, that includes your second racer.

After the first race, the player who received the lower number of points in the last race goes first. If tied, roll off!
